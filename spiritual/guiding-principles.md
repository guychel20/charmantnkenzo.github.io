# Guiding Principles

## 1. Gather Together in One All Things in Christ

My central spiritual-professional principle is derived from Elder David A. Bednar's rope analogy: true strength comes from joining related strands into a unified whole. Rather than treating spiritual practices, learning, service, and technical work as separate checkboxes, I intentionally link doctrine and practice so my professional decisions are expressions of discipleship. Practically, this means designing systems, training documents, and workflows that serve the welfare of individuals and society, not merely optimizing metrics. Integration guides priorities, informs stakeholder decisions, and frames success in covenant terms: service first, technical excellence second.

- 3 Nephi 13:19-22

## 2. Single-Eyed Devotion and Ethical Stewardship

3 Nephi 13:22's teaching on the "single eye" guides me to prioritize integrity over recognition and long-term stewardship over short-term visibility. In IT leadership, this principle translates into enforcing least privilege, insisting on secure configurations even when quick fixes seem attractive, and prioritizing privacy and user dignity over performance metrics. The "single eye" is a decision filter: will this choice honor covenants and protect the vulnerable, or will it chase attention and convenience? It shapes hiring, mentoring, and incident-response decisions.

### Initial supporting materials
- 3 Nephi 13:19-22
- President Russell M. Nelson, *Make Time for the Lord*
- Don't Take the Temple for Granted
- Blessings of the Temple

## 3. Covenant-Centered Self-Reliance and Service

Temple covenants and the Church's emphasis on self-reliance frame professional competency as sacred stewardship. Developing technical skill is a way to magnify responsibility and bless others. I aim to combine continuous skill improvement with mentorship and community service. Skills are not ends but tools for consecrated work, implemented through teaching, creating accessible resources, and strengthening institutional resilience.
